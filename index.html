Data Analisis

Methodology of the study 

To conduct a spatial analysis of rainfall intensity, distribution of water bodies, and the efficiency of the drainage area in Veracruz city to identify the primary causes of urban flooding and inform future flood mitigation strategies.

Figure 2 shows the distribution of rainfall in Veracruz city and nearby places.


Conclusions

Our objective was to create an innovative model of our community by utilizing a spatial representation of the city of Veracruz map. By incorporating open-source scientific datasets, we established relationships between the drainage system, water bodies, precipitation, and flood-prone areas. These data allowed us to determine a variety of critical aspects for environmental management and urban planning, such as:

  • Flood Risk: Combining precipitation, water body, and flood-prone area data, we can identify high-risk zones and plan mitigation measures.

  • Water Management: Drainage and water body data aid in water resource management, ensuring adequate supply and controlling the risk of droughts and floods.

  • Climate Change Impact: Precipitation and land use data can be used to model and predict the impacts of climate change on different regions.

  • Ecosystem Conservation: Identifying critical areas for biodiversity and planning conservation strategies based on land use and the presence of water bodies.

    • Due to this set of problems arising from the surrounding physical geography, we have decided to create public awareness campaigns and collaborate on management projects with the government.

Solutions

Community-Based Initiatives:

Preventive Actions Against Urban Pollution Implement comprehensive campaigns aimed at reducing urban pollution to mitigate the risk of clogged drainage systems. Strategies may include:

  • Public education programs on proper waste disposal

  • Community-based clean-up events

  • Incentives for sustainable practices

  • Community-Led Drainage Maintenance. Organize community-driven initiatives to maintain and clean drainage systems, reducing the likelihood of blockages and flooding.

Environmental Education in Schools. Develop a comprehensive environmental education curriculum for schools, focusing on the causes of flooding and the importance of preventive measures. This may involve:

  • Interactive lessons and workshops

  • Field trips to local water bodies

  • Student-led research projects

Government-Led Management Projects:

  • Early Warning Systems. Establish a robust early warning system to alert the public of impending flood events. Key components may include:Real-time flood monitoring and forecasting, multi-channel communication systems (e.g., sirens, mobile apps), community-based early warning networks

  • Green Infrastructure Development. Promote the implementation of green infrastructure solutions to enhance stormwater management and reduce flood risk. Specific initiatives may include: Green roofs, rain gardens, bioswales, urban forests.
